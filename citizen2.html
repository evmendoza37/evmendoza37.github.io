Is Citizenship the Right Choice for Me?

The process in order to become a citizen is long and difficult, but is it really necessary to become a citizen in order to thrive in the United States? The honest truth, no, but there are so many benefits that you should consider! Becoming a citizen will guaratantee more civil rights and will definitely benefit your future generations.

Advantages:
Becoming a US Citizen is a great option alongside, being a lawful permanent resident(green card holder). Here is key positive things about becoming a naturalized US citizen.

‣Voting

Only citizens can vote in federal elections. You will be able to have a say in so many decisions of the government.

‣Jury

Along with making decisions in political situations, you will have the ability to serve on the jury,which is actually the most important responsibility as a US citizen.

‣US Passport

Obtaining a US passport enables you to get assistance from the U.S. government when overseas, if necessary.

‣Bring Your Family to the US

U.S. citizens generally get priority when petitioning to bring family members permanently to this country.

‣Your Children under 18 Will be Citizens

In most cases, a child born abroad to a U.S. citizen is automatically a U.S. citizen.

‣Federal Jobs

Generally, most governmental agencies(such as Police, Firefighter, or Politician) require U.S> citizenship

‣Maintain Residency

A US citizen's right to remain in the US cannot be taken away.

‣Scholarships and Grants

Many financial aid grants (college scholarships and funds) are more available to US citizens only. For more infomation on scholarships and financial aid that you could be eligible for check go to our SCHOOL/CAREERpage.

Citizen Rights and Responsibilities Advantages and Disadvantages to US Citizenship

Disadvantages:
Becoming a US citizen is beneficial but the process and responsibilites behind it are not always dandy. Here is key negative things about becoming a naturalized US citizen.

‣Taxes

All citizens are required to pay taxes for income earned anywhere in the world. So if you plan to work outside the US, make sure you contact a tax expert to see how becoming a citizen might affect your income.
It is often hard to escape your tax liability to the US goverment, even if you give up your citizenship. Whereas, those with a green card don't have to worry about filing tax returns once they give up their green card.

‣Jury

To most, jury duty is a burden, where others feel like it is their libery and right to have the ability to do so. If jury duty is a advantage or disadvantage that is up for you to decide.

‣Losing Another Country's Citizenship

Depending on the country you currently have citizenship, becoming a 'US' citizen can change or manipulate the privileges of being in your home country.
